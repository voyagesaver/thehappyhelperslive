THe Benefits of Learning English

What will you gain?


Learning English:

Where do you want to study? Have you ever considered studying abroad? Countries like the United States and the United Kingdom are home to some of the world's most greatest universities and colleges. And even in non-English speaking countries, there are thousands of good schools around the globe offering their programs in English. Which means learning English might mean accessing a better education.

The English language is currently the most frequently used and widely spoken language in the world. One in every 5 individuals has the ability to speak or understand English. Learning English increases your chances of achieving success in a dream career, facilities in professional development, networking, and gives you the possibility to participate freely in English conversations and discussions. Most notably, English will help to increase your future educational and professional opportunities.

English is the primary language used for business. It is therefore paramount that you have a working knowledge and understanding of English – particularly when speaking to international colleagues, customers, or clients. English can also allow you to expand your company or business, even while working abroad in a non-English speaking country.

Widely Spoken
When considering why learning English is important, this is perhaps the greatest factor you ought to consider. One in five individuals in the entire world can speak, or at least understand, English. It is particularly useful for both travel and communication.

Career Booster
As the primary language of business, it is important – unequivocally – to know how to speak English when speaking with international colleagues or customers. Having English language skills increases your chances of earning a good job, either in a multi-national company or from abroad.

Easy to Learn
The vocabulary and pronunciation in English can be difficult to learn. However, English grammar, in comparison to other Western languages, is relatively easy. For instance, there are only two genders, masculine and feminine. Additionally, there is only one definite and indefinite article, and the rules are relatively simple and straightforward.

Valuable for Education
Where do you want to study? Have you considered studying abroad? The United States and the United Kingdom are home to some of the world’s greatest universities. Non-English-speaking countries also possess some of the world’s greatest schools and programs – although these options are often in English. Thus, it is recommended that you learn English in order to access a higher quality education.

Rich in Literature and Popular Culture
Have you ever heard a song and wished you could understand the lyrics? Have you ever been frustrated by a poor dub of a television show or movie? Perhaps you have desired to experience and understand the masterful works of English writers, including J.K. Rowling, Stephen King, and Jane Austen. If you relate to these, then you ought to learn English. You will finally be able to understand and relate to your favorite books, movies, television shows, and songs in their untapped and original form.

Additional Notes
- About 1,500,000,000 people in the world speak English. Another 1,000,000,000 are learning it.
- 75% of the world’s letters and postcards are written in English.
- Almost all international conferences and competitions are conducted in English. For example, The Olympics and The Miss World contests.
- Diplomats and politicians from different countries use English to communicate with one another. English is the main language of organizations including The United Nations, NATO, and the European Free Trade Association.

If you can communicate in English, you can:

Contact people from around the world. Talk about your ideas and opinions on Internet discussion groups. Send e-mails to interesting people. Learn about their lives and their culture. Travel more easily. Communicate with people wherever you go — English is spoken in more than 100 countries. Ask directions, have a conversation, ask for help. Who knows, perhaps English will save your life!

Push Your Career Forward
If you want a good job in business, technology, or science, learn English today! If you already have a good job, the benefits of learning English are astronomical.

Knowing English will let you …
- Put “excellent knowledge of English” on your CV. Get your dream job and earn more money.
- Gain technical knowledge. English is the language of technology, especially in “high technology” like computer science, genetics, and medicine. If you’re going to read about technology, you will most likely have to do it in English.
- Learn computer science. Read technical articles without difficulty or write your own articles! 
- Be a world-class businessman (or -woman). International business is conducted English, and all business today is international. If you want to play a role, you must know English. It provides you with the ability to contact other businesspeople, attend conferences, read international business newspapers and magazines.
- Become a better scientist. Contact scientists from other countries, attend international conferences, visit academic centers abroad. Learn about new scientific discoveries by reading papers, books, and magazines.
- Use your computer more effectively. Most computer applications are in English, so you will understand these applications more easily and become a better employee with your increased knowledge and skills.
- Learn new skills for your job. The section “Get access to knowledge” explains how English can help you learn.
- Enjoy art like never before. English allows you to understand and connect to the cultures of the world like no other language. With a strong, working knowledge of the English language, you will gain new experiences, which are currently not offered in other languages.
- Watch American and British films in the original. Once you try it, you’ll never go back
to dubbed versions!
